Brendan Eich


Background


Brendan Eich was born on born July 4, 1961 in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, United States. He attended Ellwood P. Cubberley High School before studying mathematics and computer science at Santa Clara University, later earning his bachelor's degree in those subjects. Even further in his life he received his master's degree in 1985 from the University of Illinois at Urbana–Champaign.
Brendan is still alive to this day, aged 60. He's married a woman named Eleanor, whom he's mad many children with.


Career

He began his career as a computer scientist at Silicon Graphics, working for seven years on operating system and network code. Afterwards, he worked at MicroUnity Systems Engineering writing microkernel and DSP code. After three years of working there, he decided to begin working at Netscape Communications Corporation in April 1995. This is where his decisions and work made a lot of change in the computer science field.

Accomplishments

After entering Netscape Communications Corporation, he had his intentions set on developping a new language for the browser their corporation was working on. Throughout his research and testing, he created a language that had much of the functionality of Scheme, the object-orientation of Self, and the syntax of Java. Later he decided to name it Javascript. Brendan Eich was the founder/creator of the well-known language. "JavaScript" some of us use on a daily basis. He also designed the very first SpiderMonkey engine for the Netscape Navigator browser.
Brendon co-founded the free and open source software project Mozilla with Jamie Zawinski after the Netscape Communications Corporation base code got transferred to the Mozilla corperation. after serving as a lead technologist in Mozilla, he got promoted to chief technical officer in 2005.
Brendan is also the CEO of Brave Software, an Internet browser platform company that was released in 2016 still to this current day. Not only is he the CEO but he also co-created the Basic Attention Token (BAT), a cryptocurrency designed for use in the Brave browser.
